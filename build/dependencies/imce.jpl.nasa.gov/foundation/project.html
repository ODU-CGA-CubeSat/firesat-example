Abstract

The Project Ontology defines concepts and properties for describing a project in terms of its stakeholders, concerns, work breakdown, and deliverables. It provides a basis for specialization by domain experts.

3. Aspects

3.1. AssignedElement

An AssignedElement is any model element that can have the isAssignedTo relationship with a Role .

Super entities: analysis:AnalyzedElement, analysis:CharacterizedElement

Specializations: Delivers, DevelopedElement, Executes, Process, Receives, ReliesOn, Supplies

Relations having AssignedElement as range:

  • Role hasAssignment AssignedElement

3.2. DevelopedElement

A DevelopedElement is any model element that manifests DevelopmentState s. Example DevelopedElement s include spacecraft, thermal test facility, and requirements document.

Super entities: analysis:AnalyzedElement, AssignedElement, analysis:CharacterizedElement

Specializations: mission:Component, Facility, mission:Interface, mission:Junction, Product

Relations having DevelopedElement as domain:

  • DevelopedElement manifests DevelopmentState

Relations having DevelopedElement as range:

  • Product describes DevelopedElement

  • Authority reliesOn DevelopedElement

3.3. RealizedElement

A RealizedElement is any element that realizes or isRealizedBy another element.

Super entities: analysis:AnalyzedElement, analysis:CharacterizedElement

Specializations: mission:Component, mission:Function, mission:Interface, mission:Junction, mission:Mission, Process, Project, WorkPackage

Relations having RealizedElement as domain:

  • RealizedElement realizes RealizedElement

Relations having RealizedElement as range:

  • RealizedElement realizes RealizedElement

3.4. SuppliedElement

A SuppliedElement is any model element that an Authority properly supplies . Example SuppliedElement s include Mission and Component .

Super entities: analysis:AnalyzedElement, analysis:CharacterizedElement

Specializations: mission:Component, mission:Mission, Product

Relations having SuppliedElement as range:

  • Authority supplies SuppliedElement

4. Concepts

4.1. Authority

An Authority is any entity that asserts a fact in a model. Example Authority s include Program , Project , and WorkPackage . (The association of an

Authority with its asserted facts requires mechanisms outside the vocabulary of OWL itself, e.g., named graphs. For this reason, there is no object property authorizes . Authorization is conveyed by the organization of statements in a repository.)

Super entities: base:Package

Specializations: Program, Project, WorkPackage

Relations having Authority as domain:

  • Authority authorizes Authority

  • Authority executes Process

  • Authority produces Product

  • Authority reliesOn DevelopedElement

  • Authority supplies SuppliedElement

Relations having Authority as range:

  • Authority authorizes Authority

  • Organization hasResponsibilityFor Authority

4.2. Concern

A Concern represents a specific interest that one or more stakeholders have in the successful completion of a Project or Program and its Mission s.

Super entities: base:AggregatedElement, base:IdentifiedElement, analysis:MeasuredElement

Specializations: mission:Objective

Supertype Relations having Concern as domain:

  • Concern aggregates AggregatedElement

Relations having Concern as range:

  • Stakeholder represents Concern

Supertype Properties: base:hasAlternateName, base:hasCanonicalName, base:hasDescription, base:hasIdentifier, base:hasIndexEntry, base:hasShortName, base:hasSortKey, base:hasUuid

  • Restricts range of aggregates to be an instance of Concern

  • Restricts range of isAggregatedIn to be an instance of Concern

4.3. Deliverable

A Deliverable is the result of successful execution of a Process . It may be an input to or prerequisite for another Process .

Super entities: base:IdentifiedElement

Specializations: DevelopmentState

Relations having Deliverable as range:

  • InterchangePoint delivers Deliverable

  • Interchange hasParticipant Deliverable

  • InterchangePoint receives Deliverable

Direct Properties: isRequiredNotLaterThan

Supertype Properties: base:hasAlternateName, base:hasCanonicalName, base:hasDescription, base:hasIdentifier, base:hasIndexEntry, base:hasShortName, base:hasSortKey, base:hasUuid

4.4. DevelopmentState

A DevelopmentState is a kind of Deliverable that represents a defined condition for a DevelopedElement . DevelopmentState s may be unique to the

DevelopedElement ; example DevelopmentState s for a Product include Preliminary, Draft, and Final.

Super entities: analysis:Characterization, Deliverable

Supertype Relations having DevelopmentState as domain:

  • DevelopmentState characterizes CharacterizedElement

Relations having DevelopmentState as range:

  • DevelopedElement manifests DevelopmentState

Supertype Properties: isRequiredNotLaterThan

  • Restricts range of characterizes to be an instance of DevelopedElement

4.5. Facility

A Facility is any asset (e.g., laboratory, assembly facility, test facility, etc.) whose DevelopmentState may be a Deliverable for a project process.

Super entities: DevelopedElement, base:IdentifiedElement

Supertype Relations having Facility as domain:

  • Facility manifests DevelopmentState

Supertype Properties: base:hasAlternateName, base:hasCanonicalName, base:hasDescription, base:hasIdentifier, base:hasIndexEntry, base:hasShortName, base:hasSortKey, base:hasUuid

4.6. Interchange

An Interchange represents the intended joint behavior of a pair of Process es. It indicates that a Deliverable may be delivered by one Process and received by the other Process .

Super entities: base:IdentifiedElement

Relations having Interchange as domain:

  • Interchange hasParticipant Deliverable

  • Interchange involves Process

  • Interchange involves1 Process

  • Interchange involves2 Process

Supertype Properties: base:hasAlternateName, base:hasCanonicalName, base:hasDescription, base:hasIdentifier, base:hasIndexEntry, base:hasShortName, base:hasSortKey, base:hasUuid

4.7. InterchangePoint

Super entities: base:IdentifiedElement

Relations having InterchangePoint as domain:

  • InterchangePoint delivers Deliverable

  • InterchangePoint receives Deliverable

Relations having InterchangePoint as range:

  • Process interchangesThrough InterchangePoint

Supertype Properties: base:hasAlternateName, base:hasCanonicalName, base:hasDescription, base:hasIdentifier, base:hasIndexEntry, base:hasShortName, base:hasSortKey, base:hasUuid

4.8. Organization

An Organization is any entity that isResponsibleFor a Authority .

Super entities: analysis:AnalyzedElement, analysis:CharacterizedElement

Relations having Organization as domain:

  • Organization hasResponsibilityFor Authority

Relations having Organization as range:

  • Person belongsTo Organization

4.9. Person

A Person corresponds to an individual named person. A Person belongsTo zero or more Organization s.

Super entities: base:IdentifiedElement

Relations having Person as domain:

  • Person belongsTo Organization

  • Person hasRole Role

Supertype Properties: base:hasAlternateName, base:hasCanonicalName, base:hasDescription, base:hasIdentifier, base:hasIndexEntry, base:hasShortName, base:hasSortKey, base:hasUuid

4.10. Policy

A Policy represents a specific exercise of authority that binds one or more model elements.

Super entities: analysis:AnalyzedElement, analysis:CharacterizedElement

Relations having Policy as domain:

  • Policy binds SpecifiedElement

4.11. Process

A Process is an activity authorized by a WorkPackage in support of delivering a Component or producing a Product . Examples include design trajectory , conduct trade study , and review subsystem design .

Super entities: AssignedElement, base:IdentifiedElement, RealizedElement

Relations having Process as domain:

  • Process interchangesThrough InterchangePoint

  • Process invokes Process

Supertype Relations having Process as domain:

  • Process realizes RealizedElement

Relations having Process as range:

  • Authority executes Process

  • Process invokes Process

  • Interchange involves Process

  • Interchange involves1 Process

  • Interchange involves2 Process

Supertype Properties: base:hasAlternateName, base:hasCanonicalName, base:hasDescription, base:hasIdentifier, base:hasIndexEntry, base:hasShortName, base:hasSortKey, base:hasUuid

  • Restricts range of realizes to be an instance of Process

  • Restricts range of isRealizedBy to be an instance of Process

4.12. Product

A Product is a document or other artifact supplied by a WorkPackage . Example Product s include trade study reports, design documents, and inspection reports.

Super entities: base:AggregatedElement, DevelopedElement, base:IdentifiedElement, SuppliedElement

Specializations: fse:Drawing

Relations having Product as domain:

  • Product describes DevelopedElement

Supertype Relations having Product as domain:

  • Product aggregates AggregatedElement

  • Product manifests DevelopmentState

Relations having Product as range:

  • Role approves Product

  • Role concurs Product

  • Role prepares Product

  • Authority produces Product

Supertype Properties: base:hasAlternateName, base:hasCanonicalName, base:hasDescription, base:hasIdentifier, base:hasIndexEntry, base:hasShortName, base:hasSortKey, base:hasUuid

  • Restricts range of aggregates to be an instance of Product

  • Restricts range of isAggregatedIn to be an instance of Product

4.13. Program

Super entities: Authority

Supertype Relations having Program as domain:

  • Program authorizes Authority

  • Program executes Process

  • Program produces Product

  • Program reliesOn DevelopedElement

  • Program supplies SuppliedElement

4.14. Project

A Project is a kind of Authority that supplies a related set of Mission s in pursuit of a set of Objective s.

Super entities: Authority, RealizedElement

Supertype Relations having Project as domain:

  • Project authorizes Authority

  • Project executes Process

  • Project produces Product

  • Project realizes RealizedElement

  • Project reliesOn DevelopedElement

  • Project supplies SuppliedElement

  • Restricts range of realizes to be an instance of Project

  • Restricts range of isRealizedBy to be an instance of Project

  • Restricts range of supplies to be an instance of Mission

4.15. Role

A Role corresponds to a set of assignments meant to be filled by a single Person .

Super entities: base:IdentifiedElement

Relations having Role as domain:

  • Role approves Product

  • Role concurs Product

  • Role delegatesTo Role

  • Role hasAssignment AssignedElement

  • Role prepares Product

Relations having Role as range:

  • Role delegatesTo Role

  • Person hasRole Role

Supertype Properties: base:hasAlternateName, base:hasCanonicalName, base:hasDescription, base:hasIdentifier, base:hasIndexEntry, base:hasShortName, base:hasSortKey, base:hasUuid

4.16. Stakeholder

A Stakeholder is a person or organization with a recognized interest in the successful completion of a Project or Program . Example

Stakeholder s include scientists, NASA Directorates, nuclear safety advocates, and the general public.

Super entities: base:IdentifiedElement

Relations having Stakeholder as domain:

  • Stakeholder represents Concern

Supertype Properties: base:hasAlternateName, base:hasCanonicalName, base:hasDescription, base:hasIdentifier, base:hasIndexEntry, base:hasShortName, base:hasSortKey, base:hasUuid

4.17. WorkPackage

A WorkPackage represents a discrete unit of Project cost, schedule, and activity. WorkPackage s form a hierarchy called the Work Breakdown Structure.

Super entities: Authority, base:Package, RealizedElement

Supertype Relations having WorkPackage as domain:

  • WorkPackage authorizes Authority

  • WorkPackage executes Process

  • WorkPackage produces Product

  • WorkPackage realizes RealizedElement

  • WorkPackage reliesOn DevelopedElement

  • WorkPackage supplies SuppliedElement

  • Restricts range of isRealizedBy to be an instance of WorkPackage

  • Restricts range of realizes to be an instance of WorkPackage

  • Restricts range of supplies to be an instance of Component

Reverse: isBoundBy See binds .

Attributes: InverseFunctional, Asymmetric, Irreflexive

Source: Role

Target: Role

Reverse: isDelegateOf See delegatesTo .

Super terms: analysis:AnalyzedElement, analysis:CharacterizedElement

6.7. Delivers

Attributes: InverseFunctional, Asymmetric, Irreflexive

Source: InterchangePoint

Target: Deliverable

Reverse: isDeliveredBy See delivers .

Super terms: analysis:AnalyzedElement, AssignedElement, analysis:CharacterizedElement

6.8. Describes

Attributes: Asymmetric, Irreflexive

Reverse: isDescribedBy See describes .

Super terms: analysis:AnalyzedElement, analysis:CharacterizedElement

6.9. Executes

Attributes: InverseFunctional, Asymmetric, Irreflexive

Reverse: isExecutedBy See executes .

Super terms: AssignedElement, mission:SpecifiedElement

6.10. HasAssignment

Attributes: Asymmetric, Irreflexive

Reverse: isAssignedTo See hasAssignment .

Super terms: analysis:AnalyzedElement, analysis:CharacterizedElement

Sub terms: Approves, Concurs, Prepares

6.12. HasResponsibilityFor

Attributes: InverseFunctional

Source: Organization

Target: Authority

Forward: hasResponsibilityFor An Organization o hasResponsibilityFor some Authority a if and only if o provides the actual workforce and execution of the work embodied in a . An example is the relation between a "doing"

Organization and a WorkPackage .

Reverse: isResponsibilityOf See hasResponsibilityFor .

Super terms: analysis:AnalyzedElement, analysis:CharacterizedElement

Scalar value type: date
